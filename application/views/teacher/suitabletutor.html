How to find a suitable tutor

Finding a competent tutor can be exhausting. The possibilities are manifold and many tutoring providers have supposed advantages. Unfortunately, one has to pay a lot of attention , because otherwise either too much money is paid or the school achievements of the child do not improve as desired .

Find teachers in classifieds

The first port of call, when your own child brings bad grades home, are mostly tutoring on classified ad portals. Here, the selection is often very large and everyone tries to present as well as possible. The references often can not check , so you decide to "herumgeklicke" and "herumgewische" for a tutor you are comfortable with quite nice for an eternal. Contacting the "teacher" and need to find first a suitable date, The tutor will then appear on the agreed date and the daughter or son sit with him alone in a room. After 90 minutes the private tutoring lesson is over and the teacher has to be paid, which usually costs you 30 to 40 €. For private tuition there is unfortunately no possibility of a free trial lesson in most cases , because nobody will make the long journey to your home to provide free tuition. It's also difficult to tell if this person is good at teaching. If your child is dissatisfied with the teacher, you must search again.

Beware of untested teachers!

Anyone can pretend to be a tutor on the Internet, who has allegedly given private lessons for years. But have you ever had a certificate from a tutor show ? Do you know if this teacher, who asks 20 euros per hour for the tutoring really has a clue of what he is doing there? Both are answered in most cases with "no". And in the end, the grade of your child does not improve, dates are postponed and / or canceled and after a few months, it all starts again - you are looking for a new teacher! The time required and the associated frustration, especially when the grade of her child has not improved in the tutoring, it was not the supposed savings.

Intermediation platforms for tutors

An alternative to simple classified ads are platforms that teach teachers. The only difference is that they promise that the tutors will be checked for quality . But if you look more closely at how such portals are built and which business model is behind it, you quickly realize that this promise is not kept . There are many platforms where the tutor needs to pay a monthly fee so that they can appear on the website and be contacted by the student. Hardly anyone deals with checking the teachers. As a rule, every registration is accepted as it is earn as many teachers as possible on the website .

There are also portals that promise you to pay only 12-14 € per hour for one-to-one tuition. This payment should then go directly to the teacher. However, if you call there, you will be told to pay an agency fee of up to € 80 . If you would like to change a teacher, you will pay a fee again. Thus, all flexibility and alleged savings are completely removed.

Classic tutoring at the institute on site - the best solution?

If you want to "play it safe", then many opt for a tutoring around the corner. Because why worry about tuition? You want to change the condition of a bad grade as soon as possible, and preferably immediately and not in 2 years ! This works best with the well-known institutes, they have the experience and do not gamble on a certain, right? Unfortunately, the rule is that long contracts must be completed to get a "tutoring from 8 €". These contracts are linked to an unnecessary handling fee and the obligation 3 times a week To take lessons. Then your child has to drive to the institute every week at the same time completely listless and demotivated, where it is put in a room with felt 10 other tuition students from different grades.

Who decides against the cheap group help, also finds providers who offer private lessons for the home, but this is usually priceless and costs depending on the term of the contract at least 40 € per lesson.

And what do you do if you also need tuition in another subject? This is very problematic for instutites, because the contract is usually only for a specific subject or a private teacher and for the desired flexibility is then a hefty fee due . Yes, this kind of tutoring should be avoided!

Online tuition - can this work at all?

For online tutoring, you usually think of the learning videos on Youtube, which have been pushed up quite a bit in the media lately. Does that make sense for your child? If it is good to be able to learn alone and is self-employed, then maybe. But the topics are treated very superficially and individual problems and questions are not discussed . In other words, they are books 2.0, for which you pay even monthly for some providers.

Would not it be better if you were personally supported online by a real person ? Many parents would now say "But teaching over the Internet can not work properly, how should my child then work with the tutor tasks? " Yes, online tutoring has not worked properly for a long time and the student was forced to just watch him could not handle anything during the tutorial and children had no idea how it all worked.

However, these times have changed. The kids are better acquainted with the technology than the parents, and modern programs, which until now have been used only by companies like Easy-Tutor, are so far that not only the teacher is active, but also the student . Working together on tasks, even though you are hundreds of kilometers away from each other, is no longer an obstacle thanks to state-of-the-art software . The student does not necessarily need a PC, nor does he have to have studied computer science before to be able to handle the program. These programs are intuitive and to use both on the PC as well as on the tablet or smartphone. And please, what child over the age of 12 does not have a smartphone these days? The possibilities are manifold and by far the biggest advantage is the sensible use of these media. They should not just serve entertainment.
